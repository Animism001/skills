# 罗伯特·麦基长对话、播客、深度采访调研

## 1. 被追问时的回答方式

### 示例1：关于"否定之否定"概念的澄清
**内容：当被采访者追问"否定之否定"概念时，麦基没有直接回避，而是先坦诚自己早期没有在讲座中没有充分解释清楚，然后主动补充说明：
> "I don't know when you took my lecture, but when you did, I suspect that I didn't actually explain how you get to the negation of the negation. I'll do that now."

随后详细解释了两种技巧，区分了量变质变的区别。

**来源**：https://www.vice.com/en/article/the-god-of-story-0000339-v21n6
**可信度**：高
**一手/二手**：一手
**备注**：VICE杂志2014年深度采访

### 示例2：对"电影是声明，电视是对话"的回应
**内容**：当被采访者提出"Movies are statements, and television is conversation"这个观点时，麦基直接回应：
> "That's nonsense. I don't know what the fuck that means."

随后进一步阐述自己的观点：
> "Television is just one huge, complex statement."

**来源**：https://www.vice.com/en/article/the-god-of-story-0000339-v21n6
**可信度**：高
**一手/二手**：一手
**备注**：VICE杂志2014年深度采访

### 示例3：关于好莱坞编剧课程演进的回应
**内容**：被问及课程如何随时间演进时，麦基解释：
> "What's happened is that people have asked questions over the years. When I hear the same question enough times, I realize that there's a hole in the lecture. Then I build a section into the lecture around the question to preempt it."

**来源**：https://www.vice.com/en/article/the-god-of-story-0000339-v21n6
**可信度**：高
**一手/二手**：一手
**备注**：VICE杂志2014年深度采访

## 2. 即兴类比

### 类比1：创造力限制原则 - 口哨 vs 贝多芬交响曲
**内容**：
> "Why is whistling not a Beethoven symphony? Because whistling is easy. A Beethoven symphony is hard. But when you take on the challenge of writing a symphony, the creative solutions are amazing, overwhelming. Whistling is something you can do on the street. The more difficult the technique, the more brilliant the solution."

**来源**：https://stevekayser.com/principle-of-creative-limitation/
**可信度**：高
**一手/二手**：一手
**备注**：Steve Kayser对麦基的专访

### 类比2：创造力限制原则 - 高尔夫 vs 乒乓球
**内容**：
> "Another analogy, golf is harder than ping-pong. It's not that ping-pong isn't good, it's a lot of fun, and at the highest levels, it's wonderful. But ping-pongers are not Tiger Woods, Why? Because the golf swing is infinitely more difficult than hitting a ping-pong ball."

**来源**：https://stevekayser.com/principle-of-creative-limitation/
**可信度**：高
**一手/二手**：一手
**备注**：Steve Kayser对麦基的专访

### 类比3：人生不是旅程，而是挣扎
**内容**：
> "A story isn't a journey. That's a California word, a euphemism used to not face reality. We don't want to use the word 'shit' we use the word 'number 2' but you people are writers and you don't live in euphemism. Life isn't a journey. It's a struggle. It's Sisyphus dragging the rock up the mountain. A bus ride is a journey. You get to sit comfortably and get to the other side. Life isn't like that. It's hard."

**来源**：https://londonscreenwritersfestival.com/why-do-you-write-and-other-questions-from-robert-mckee-by-cassi-camilleri/
**可信度**：高
**一手/二手**：二手（现场报道）
**备注**：伦敦编剧节2020年大师班现场报道

### 类比4：故事是宇宙，神话是其中一部分
**内容**：
> "The monomyth implies that it is the only story. False. Story is the universe. Myth is a part within it. People mistook the part for the whole."

**来源**：https://londonscreenwritersfestival.com/why-do-you-write-and-other-questions-from-robert-mckee-by-cassi-camilleri/
**可信度**：高
**一手/二手**：二手（现场报道）
**备注**：伦敦编剧节2020年大师班现场报道

### 类比5：人物像心理分析师
**内容**：
> "Writers can't possibly create excellent non-fiction or fiction if they're not aware of what is going on inside of other people, even subconsciously, while they go about saying whatever they do consciously in the world. Because if you just recorded the surface, you'd be missing the whole show."

**来源**：https://thestory.au/articles/four-storytelling-tips-from-robert-mckee/
**可信度**：中
**一手/二手**：二手（基于Longform播客采访整理）
**备注**：The Story网站整理自Longform播客448期

## 3. 改变立场的瞬间

### 示例1：对《Waterworld》的看法转变
**内容**：
> "After all the bad-mouthing of Waterworld, and a few other films I could think of, I'm sitting in a hotel room in the middle of the night, suffering jet lag, and there's Waterworld. And it's not that bad! Waterworld was actually pretty good."

这表明麦基从最初的负面看法转变为比较正面的评价。

**来源**：https://www.vice.com/en/article/the-god-of-story-0000339-v21n6
**可信度**：高
**一手/二手**：一手
**备注**：VICE杂志2014年深度采访

### 示例2：从早期对约瑟夫·坎贝尔的立场（可能的转变
**内容**：
早期在《Story》书中，麦基借用了坎贝尔的一些观点，但在后来的讲座中激烈批评：
> "Joseph Campbell LIED," McKee emphasises. "There is no such thing as a monomyth. Anthropologists since Campbell have pointed out repeatedly that this is a lie. Campbell hated Christianity and wanted to create the foundation for a new religion."

这表明从早期的借用转变为后来的强烈批评。

**来源**：
- http://www.williammistele.com/mckee.htm（早期观点）
- https://londonscreenwritersfestival.com/why-do-you-write-and-other-questions-from-robert-mckee-by-cassi-camilleri/（后期批评）
**可信度**：高
**一手/二手**：一手（后期）/ 二手（早期）
**备注**：显示出明显的立场转变

### 示例3：讲座内容的演进
**内容**：
> "The lecture was 24 hours originally, and I showed both Casablanca and Chinatown. I eliminated Chinatown to gain four hours, and the original 24 hours has built up to 32 hours over four days."

这表明麦基对讲座内容进行了持续的调整和演进。

**来源**：https://www.vice.com/en/article/the-god-of-story-0000339-v21n6
**可信度**：高
**一手/二手**：一手
**备注**：VICE杂志2014年深度采访

## 4. 拒绝回答的问题

### 示例1：奇怪问题的处理
**内容**：当被问及"最奇怪的问题"时，麦基没有直接回答具体问题，而是说：
> "The weird questions come from people who are a little weird to begin with. They often come from another discipline, like science or physics or chemistry or astrology."

然后模糊地描述了这些问题的类型，但没有具体回答。

**来源**：https://www.vice.com/en/article/the-god-of-story-0000339-v21n6
**可信度**：高
**一手/二手**：一手
**备注**：VICE杂志2014年深度采访

### 示例2：关于罗杰·兔子是否是唐人街三部曲第三部的问题
**内容**：
> "I don't know, but they're all about the red cars."

没有直接确认或否认，只是模糊回应。

**来源**：https://www.vice.com/en/article/the-god-of-story-0000339-v21n6
**可信度**：高
**一手/二手**：一手
**备注**：VICE杂志2014年深度采访

### 示例3：对心理学的兴趣
**内容**：在早期的一个问答中，被问及对心理学的兴趣时：
> "Yes, my work is ahead of psychology. But why bother with psychology? Someone else will have to write a book about that. My interest is in art."

巧妙地将问题转移到艺术领域，没有深入回答心理学相关问题。

**来源**：http://www.williammistele.com/mckee.htm
**可信度**：中
**一手/二手**：一手
**备注**：William Mistele的个人网站
